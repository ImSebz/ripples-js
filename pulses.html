Overview

We have designed Pulses in motion that are exclusively used across digital channels. Magnetic and sensorial, these moving Pulses emanate from the globe logo, and are designed to mesmerize and engage our audience across interactive touchpoints.

The Pulse travels in a uniform, concentric pattern from the center of the frame; the movements are bold, confident, and energetic. The beat of the Pulse sets the tempo for the piece. The preferred speed is 100 BPM as our logo in motion should never feel chaotic or frenetic. If a new music track or creative vision requires, the BPM may be altered given approval from the brand and design teams.

The globe logo is the heart of our brand. When our logo is in motion, it functions as the beating heart of our Visual Identity. Each pulse communicates that Pepsi is the instigator and catalyst for unapologetic moments.

The logo occupies the center of the frame and beats outwards to initiate each wave of the pulse. This Pulse library will grow as we continue to amplify the brand. Approval is required by the brand and design team if a bespoke pulse is needed.

We have created unique Pulses in 5 creative territories:

  • Graphic
  • Light
  • Music
  • Flavor & Refresh
  • Specialty


These Pulses can be used individually, multiple from the same territory, or in sequence showcasing the diverse range of our brand personality, pillars and touchpoints.

Pulses can be used as brand interstitials or as key moments. For Over Image application, we recommend using the 2D Graphic Pulses and have provided guidelines on how it should be placed. The 3D Pulses should be standalone moments and are not intended to be used under or over other graphics or footage.

Formats are available in 16:9, 1:1, 9:16, and 4:5.

The Graphic pulses are made of 2D vector dots and lines. They are inspired by our Visual Identity and the refreshing and fizzy attributes of our product. 

  • Utilizing the dot pulses is encouraged as it is ownable; lines and dots together are also available and more ownable compared to lines alone, which should be used sparingly.
  • Graphic pulses should only be used in our electric blue.
  • Graphic pulses are our preferred pulse over live footage.
design tip

The graphic pulse library will grow as we continue to amplify the brand. If a bespoke graphic Pulse is required, consult the design team. Approval is required by brand and design team.

The Specialty Pulses are created for bespoke creative needs that can be used in very specific scenarios. 

  • Specialty Pulses are usually not evergreen Pulses and should be utilized to amplify a theme. For example, we have created a vibrant confetti pulse to use as we celebrate our 125th year. This Pulse may continue to be used in similar celebratory contexts.
  • Do not change the color or texture of these Pulses.
  • Use caution when using these Pulses over image or footage, as it may lose context and vibrancy. Aim to use over simple and darker backgrounds for the best display of the 3D effects.
OVER IMAGE PULSE use
DOWNLOAD

Pulses placed over images and footage are for bespoke creative needs. They should be used to reinforce our Visual Identity and role as the instigator of unapologetic fun in multiple lifestyle touchpoints.

  • Only use the 2D graphic pulses over image and footage. The 3D pulses are not as effective and quality control is challenging.
  • Dot pulses and dots with lines pulses are preferred. It is advised not to use the line only pulse over image as past explorations have shown this is not favorable.
  • The Over Image pulse should not overly distract from the visual storyline. It should add a moment of unapologetic joy and vibrancy.
  • Use Pulses over image sparingly only in iconic moments. For example, when a can cracks open, the Pulse can be used to amplify the experience.
  • The Pulse can be used straight on, or angled with dimension in the space (eg: Pulse with can crack). This is the only instance in which dimension can be used with the Pulse.
